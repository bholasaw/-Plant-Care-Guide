Introduction

Water is one of the most essential resources for plant survival. It drives growth, nutrient absorption, and overall plant health.

Factors Affecting Water Needs

  • Plant species: succulents need less, tropicals need more.
  • Soil type & drainage: sandy soil drains fast, clay retains water.
  • Climate & seasons: heat increases demand, winter reduces it.
  • Pot size & material: clay dries faster than plastic.
  • Plant age: young plants need more frequent watering.

Watering Techniques

  • Top watering
  • Bottom watering
  • Deep watering
  • Mist spraying
  • Self-watering systems

Best Practices for Watering

  • Water in the morning for better absorption.
  • Check soil moisture before watering.
  • Use room-temperature water.
  • Avoid stressing plants by irregular watering.

Signs of Improper Watering

  • Overwatering: yellow leaves, root rot.
  • Underwatering: drooping leaves, dry soil.
  • How to revive stressed plants: rehydrate gradually and adjust routine.
